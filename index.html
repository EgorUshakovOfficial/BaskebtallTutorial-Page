types of shots

There are three types of basketball shots: layups, mid-range shots, and three-point shots.

In this page, basic steps taken to shoot mid-range shots and three-point shots are discussed.

These steps are:

  • Balance
  • Eyes
  • Elbow
  • Followthrough
  • Short for BEEF

balance

Balance is an important step in helping beginners develop good shooting mechanics. Without proper balance, the trajectory of their shot will be off.

Here are tips to aid young hoopers in developing proper balance:

  • Make sure your feet are facing toward the basket
  • Feet should sholder-width-apart, meaning your feet should be the same width as sholders

elbow

Elbow should be in when shooting the basketball. If the hooper's elbow is slightly out, the trajectory of their shot will be off.

Here are tips to help young hoopers prevent this:

  • bend your elbow until the angle between your forearm and bicep form a 90 degree angle
  • place the ball on your fingertips
  • while keeping the ball on your fingertips and the 90 degree angle, lift the ball up to your head

eyes

When shooting the basketball, the beginners should make sure they're looking at the front of the rim, the back of the rim, or the rim as a whole and the ball itself. Bad shooting mechanics can be developed otherwise.

followthrough
